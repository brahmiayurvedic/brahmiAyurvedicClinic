How Can We Help?

Virtual Ayurveda

Online consultations through Google Meet employ prashna pariksha, a pivotal diagnostic method. It entails inquiring about patients' medical history, health, and routine, revealing disease origins like dietary or lifestyle factors. This underscores patient involvement in their healing journey.

Pratyaksha Pariksha

In-person consultations offer several advantages, including a deep understanding of one's individual constitution and imbalances. Through personalized one-on-one guidance, individuals can grasp the art of nurturing themselves in alignment with their dosha, fostering optimal well-being.

Dinacharya

Dinacharya is an Ayurvedic practice centered on daily routines for holistic well-being. This regimen involves early rising, tongue scraping, oil massages, exercise, and bathing. Embracing Dinacharya is thought to harmonize the body's natural rhythms and elevate one's health.

Rutucharya

Rutucharya is an Ayurvedic seasonal regimen, prioritizes health by aligning diet, exercise, and lifestyle with each season's distinct characteristics. This adaptation to weather, temperature, and humidity changes fosters bodily balance, wards off illnesses, and enhances overall well-being.

Yoga

Yoga, originating in India, intertwines centuries-old practices like physical postures (asanas), breath control (pranayama), and meditation to enhance well-being. It cultivates flexibility, strength, and relaxation, nurturing inner calm and self-awareness.

Ahara Paryanta

Ayurveda's diet charts and "Ahara" concept go beyond physical nourishment, playing a pivotal role in holistic balance for body and mind. This balance shapes overall well-being, underlining how food choices deeply affect physical health and emotional state.
